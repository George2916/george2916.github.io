THE LIVES OF AIDAN AND HARRISON: AN UNCLE'S PERSPECTIVE

Harrison Woodell Greenslade is born on February 15, 2002, the son of Steve and Lori Greenslade. His middle name is the maiden name of his maternal grandmother. He has two older sisters and an older brother. Famous people born on February 15th include singer/songwriter Melissa Manchester and mathematician Alfred North Whitehead.

In April 2002, Uncle George meets Harrison at his initiation into the Catholic church. Uncle George is asked to be Harrison's godfather(1), which automatically moves Harrison to first place in his uncle's list of favorite nephews.

In December 2009, Harrison and his family meet Uncle George and his family and others in Myrtle Beach to celebrate the 50th wedding anniversary of HW's paternal grandparents.(12) Harrison is not feeling well and, immediately after this picture is taken, he is carried by his paterfamilias to the bathroom in order to spew. This medical emergency accounts for the absence of Harrison and his paterfamilias from the photo from December 2009 that appears on Aidan's page.

In July 2018, Uncle George and Aunt Sunghee visit the Greenslades in Topsfield.(21) During the visit, Harrison, Aidan and Uncle George again engage in a competition to determine who can throw a football the farthest. A now pubescent Harrison again comes in first, followed by Aidan and Uncle George, a result which at this point comes as little surpise to the latter but which nonetheless engenders in him no small amount of dejection. Afterwards they repair to Five Guys for a sumptuous repast.(22)

In June 2021, Uncle George attends the high school graduation ceremony of Harrison and his future step-brother, Billie.(23)(24) To the disappointment of Uncle George, he is not able to visit Richardson's for ice cream. (Some quick and unrelated side notes: (1) Harrison's girlfriend at the time is a scion of the Richardson ice cream dynasty. (2) Apropos of nothing, Uncle George urges HW not to get a tattoo of his first girlfriend, no matter how seemingly profound his love or undying his commitment.)
